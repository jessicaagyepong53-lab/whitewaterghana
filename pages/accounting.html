Accounting & Finance

General ledger, cashbook, account summaries, and multi-currency exchange rates in one unified view.

General Ledger records every financial transaction using double-entry bookkeeping. Each entry has a debit and/or credit side.

  • Asset — Bank deposits, inventory purchases (debit increases)
  • Liability — Supplier invoices, loans received (credit increases)
  • Equity — Owner capital injections, retained earnings
  • Revenue — Cash sales, invoice payments received (credit increases)
  • Expense — Utilities, transport, salaries paid (debit increases)

For every debit there should be a matching credit entry to keep the books balanced.

Account Summary shows your company's financial position across 5 categories:

  • Assets — What the company owns (Bank, Inventory, Accounts Receivable, Equipment)
  • Liabilities — What the company owes (Accounts Payable, Loans, Accrued Expenses)
  • Equity — Owner's stake (Owner Capital, Retained Earnings)
  • Revenue — Income earned (Sales Revenue, Other Income)
  • Expenses — Costs incurred (Utilities, Transport, Salaries, Packaging)

Add accounts that match your books. Totals calculate automatically per category.

Multi-Currency Support
The system supports transactions in multiple currencies. Exchange rates are updated daily and all transactions are converted to the base currency (GHS) in the general ledger.
